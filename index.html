LEDGER · 02 DEPOSITS

Deposit Calculators

Fixed / reinvestment deposits, recurring deposits, and SIPs — each with its own maturity ledger.

Reinvestment / cumulative schemes are FDs where interest is added back to principal instead of paid out. For non-cumulative FDs: quarterly payout pays the exact simple quarterly interest (P×R/4) with principal untouched, while monthly payout pays a discounted monthly rate — since you receive the money sooner, banks pay slightly less than a plain 1/12th of the annual interest, so that 12 monthly payouts work out equivalent to 4 quarterly ones. This mirrors standard practice at Indian banks (e.g. ICICI, SBI).
Compounding-period register
Interest is compounded quarterly on the running balance, in line with standard bank RD practice; the last part-quarter (if any) earns simple interest.
